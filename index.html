Knowledge Base

Indoor Plant Care Guide

Basic rules of thumb to help your botanical friends thrive in home settings.

💧

Watering Tips

Overwatering is the number one killer of indoor houseplants. Always check the soil dampness first:

  • The Touch Test: Push your finger 1-2 inches deep into the soil. If it feels completely dry, it's time to water.
  • Succulents & Aloe: Water only once every 10-14 days. Let the soil dry out entirely between waterings.
  • Pothos & Lilies: Prefer consistently damp (but not soggy) soil. Water roughly once or twice a week.
  • Drainage: Ensure your pots have drainage holes to prevent standing water at the roots (root rot).
☀️

Sunlight Requirements

Matching light levels is critical for foliage color and plant survival:

  • Bright Direct Light: Perfect for Aloe Vera and Jade. Keep them on south-facing windowsills.
  • Bright Indirect Light: Money Plant, Spider Plant, and Bamboo love dappled light, slightly away from window glass.
  • Low Light Tolerant: Snake Plants and Peace Lilies can grow in offices, bathrooms, and darker room corners.
  • Signs of Distress: Scorched brown leaves indicate too much direct sun; leggy pale stems mean they are reaching for light.
🌱

Fertilizer Guide

Indoor plants require nutrition supplements since potting soil degrades over time:

  • Active Growing Season: Feed plants once a month from Spring through Summer (March to August).
  • Dormant Season: Reduce or stop fertilizing during Winter (November to February) as plants rest.
  • Nutrient Choice: Use a balanced, water-soluble organic liquid fertilizer diluted to half-strength.
  • Organic Alternatives: Top-dress pots with vermicompost (earthworm castings) once every 3 months for slow-release nutrients.
